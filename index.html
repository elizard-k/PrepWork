Chocolate Fudge

Description

There’s something timeless about a rich, chocolatey fudge. Add in a handful of crunchy walnuts, and you’ve got a treat that’s both indulgent and perfectly balanced. This recipe is simple enough for beginners, but the results taste like something from a classic candy shop.

Whether you’re making it for the holidays, a party, or just to satisfy a chocolate craving, this fudge will quickly become a go-to.

You’ll only need a handful of pantry staples to whip this up:

Ingredients

  • 3 cups semisweet chocolate chips (about 18 ounces)
  • 1 can sweetened condensed milk (14 ounces)
  • ½ cup unsalted butter (1 stick), cut into pieces
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract
  • 1 cup chopped walnuts
  • Pinch of salt

Steps

  1. Line an 8-inch square baking pan with parchment paper or foil, leaving some overhang on the sides. Lightly butter or spray the lining to prevent sticking.
  2. In a medium saucepan over low heat, combine the chocolate chips, condensed milk, and butter. Stir constantly until everything is melted and smooth. Be careful not to let the mixture boil.
  3. Remove the pan from the heat and stir in the vanilla extract and a pinch of salt. Fold in the walnuts until evenly mixed.
  4. Pour the mixture into the prepared pan, smoothing the top with a spatula. Let it cool at room temperature for about 1 hour, then refrigerate for at least 2 hours or until firm.
  5. Once set, lift the fudge out of the pan using the parchment overhang and cut into neat 1-inch squares.
